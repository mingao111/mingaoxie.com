Strategic Entry and Exit in the U.S. Airline Industry

The U.S. airline industry is turbulent, characterized by intense competition and the frequent addition or abandonment of thousands of routes each year. An intriguing phenomenon is the entry of small airlines into markets that have been abandoned by major carriers. These abandoned routes are typically unprofitable and have limited market demand, raising the question: why do smaller airlines pursue such seemingly unfavorable markets?
